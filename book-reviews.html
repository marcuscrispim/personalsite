Book Reviews

"Engineering Mechanics: Dynamics" by J.L. Meriam & L.G. Kraige

Rating: 4/5 stars

This textbook is a classic in mechanical engineering. The explanations of dynamics principles are thorough, with clear examples. I especially appreciated the sections on kinematics of rigid bodies, which helped me during my sophomore year. However, some problems at the end of chapters are quite challenging. "The Innovators: How a Group of Hackers, Geniuses, and Geeks Created the Digital Revolution" by Walter Isaacson

Rating: 5/5 stars

An inspiring read that, while not strictly about mechanical engineering, provides insight into the innovation process. Isaacson narrates the stories of inventors and engineers in a captivating way. "Machine Design" by Robert L. Norton

Rating: 4/5 stars

Norton's book on machine design covers a broad range of topics from gears to mechanical components design. It's a great reference for understanding practical design considerations and failure analysis. In my project work, I often refer to chapters on material selection and fatigue.
